Project 1: Bachelor Thesis - Asymptotic Normality for Tessellation-based Betti Numbers

In this project, we explore topological data analysis (TDA), a powerful tool for unraveling spatial structures in datasets. Our focus centers on persistent Betti numbers, am approach that offers a multi-scale perspective on topological changes.

Objectives:

  • Explore the computation of persistent Betti numbers.
  • Establish a central limit theorem for persistent Betti numbers for a general filtration satisfying some conditions. The conditions are, for example, met by the Cech filtration.
  • Establish another central limit theorem for persistent Betti numbers for a Voronoi tessellation-adapted filtration.

Significance: These theorems lay the groundwork for statistical analysis and hypothesis testing within computational topology. They enable us to gain deeper insights into the topological properties of datasets.

Methodology:

  • Conduct simulations to study the behavior of persistent Betti numbers under varying parameters.
  • Propose a hypothesis test to determine whether the distribution of data points follows a fixed distribution or a Poisson distribution.

Key Findings: The research showcases the effectiveness of Voronoi tessellation in analyzing the topological structure of datasets. It provides valuable insights for understanding complex data through a topological lens.

Project 2: Case study on Cox model

In this project, we do a case study on Cox model for a prostate cancer dataset from a study comparing four treatments for advanced cancer stages. The project assumes familiarity with survival analysis and the Cox model, and is instead focused on modelling and presenting results.

Objective of study

  • Primary goal was to discover patterns in survival and assess the effect of treatment adjusted for patient heterogeneity
  • Prediction is a secondary goal

Methodology

    Generally the methodology follows the recommendations given by Frank Harrell in Regression Modelling Strategies. This includes the following
  • Imputing missing values
  • Model checking
  • Describing effects and presenting the model
  • For prediction validate the model using bootstrap and adjusting model using shrinkage estimate
  • Approximating the full model
