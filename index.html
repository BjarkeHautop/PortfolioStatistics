Project 1: Bachelor Thesis - Asymptotic Normality for Tessellation-based Betti Numbers

In this project, we explore the fascinating realm of topological data analysis (TDA), a powerful tool for unraveling the intricate structures within complex datasets. Our focus centers on persistent Betti numbers, am approach that offers a multi-scale perspective on topological changes.

Objectives:

  • Explore the computation of persistent Betti numbers.
  • Establish a central limit theorem for persistent Betti numbers for a general filtration satisfying some conditions. The conditions are, for example, met by the Cech filtration.
  • Establish another central limit theorem for persistent Betti numbers for a Voronoi tessellation-adapted filtration.

Significance: These theorems lay the groundwork for statistical analysis and hypothesis testing within computational topology. They enable us to gain deeper insights into the topological properties of datasets.

Methodology:

  • Conduct simulations to study the behavior of persistent Betti numbers under varying parameters.
  • Propose a hypothesis test to determine whether the distribution of data points follows a fixed distribution or a Poisson distribution.

Key Findings: The research showcases the effectiveness of Voronoi tessellation in analyzing the topological structure of datasets. It provides valuable insights for understanding complex data through a topological lens.

Project 2: Improving performance of R's function rgeom

In this project, I focused on optimizing the performance of R's built-in function rgeom, which is used for sampling from a geometric distribution. Geometric distribution plays a crucial role in various statistical and probabilistic applications, and having an efficient implementation is essential.

Objectives:

  • Develop a faster algorithm for sampling from the geometric distribution by utilizing the inverse transform method.
  • Benchmark the newly developed algorithm against R's native rgeom function to assess performance improvements.
  • Ensure consistency in output and handling of invalid inputs to match the behavior of rgeom.

Key Achievements:

  • Implemented a new my_rgeom function that outperforms R's rgeom by approximately 30%, making it a valuable addition for users working with geometric distributions.
  • Ensured that my_rgeom maintains the same output behavior and data type handling as rgeom, providing a seamless transition for users.

Significance: This project contributes to the R programming community by offering an improved and faster alternative for generating random numbers from the geometric distribution. It enhances the overall efficiency of statistical simulations and analyses.
